Shivani Shedde is a practicing architect and graduate student at the Yale School of Architecture's Master of Environmental Design Program. Her work is concerned with State-mandated patterns of (knowledge) production and consumption that facilitate both environmental and social change through the management of space. Her current research on the gold mining industry in South India continues this investigation through the social contract formed between State, the environment and its citizens. Shivani is from Mumbai, India, where she received a Bachelor of Architecture as well as a Diploma in Modern and Contemporary Art History. She is the recipient of the Yale Centre for British Art's Paul Mellon Travel Grant as well as the Yale Macmillan Centre's Rustgi Fellowship.
